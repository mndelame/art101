Lab 4 - Pseudocode and Problem-Solving

Challenge

Pseudocode and Problem-solving. 4 tasks in which you create a psuedocode for a simple tasks, then psuedocode for a simple computer game, turning the psuedocode into a comments, then creating an index.html for lab 4.

Problems

Personally for me, before even starting the lab I think understanding how to even psuedocode something basic, or honestly anything was something that was hard for me. Being able to approach a situation while planning it out in various in-depth steps was very interesting to do, especially since knowing I would have to then make it into a "coding language" was very intimidating to look at.

Reflection

I think this lab went fairly well. I think I had a lot more struggle with how to describe steps of something, but my partner was very helpful. Results

Task 1: How to make tea: - Take out a tea bag, cup, coffee stick, and kettle. - Fill the kettle with water. - Turn on kettle until water is boiling. - Place tea bag into the cup. - Pour the boiling water into the cup. - Wait 3-5 minutes. - Add sugar or honey or lemon after the 3-5 minutes if you want. - Stir the tea in a circle motion with a coffee stick. - Enjoy the tea!

Task 2: Creating a rock, paper, scissor game: 1. Get two players. 2. Have each player choose either rock, paper, or scissors. 3. At the same time, each player reveals what object they have chosen. If one player chooses rock and the other scissors, rock wins. If one player chooses rock and the other paper, paper wins. If both players choose rock, repeat step 2. If one player chooses scissors and the other paper, scissors wins. If both players choose scissors, repeat step 2. If both players choose paper, repeat step 2. 4. End game.
